<h2><b> <img src="https://api.iconify.design/hugeicons:artificial-intelligence-04.svg?color=%23888888" width="28"> What is LLM ? </b></h2>

LLM stands for Large Language Model, is a type of artificial intelligence (AI) that has been trained on vast amounts of text, image and documentation data to understand, generate and interact with human language.

It performs a wide variety of tasks such as:
- Answering Questions
- Summarizing long and large documents
- Translating between languages
- Writing essays, stories, vibe coding, music creation, video editing and many more
- Brainstorming ideas
- Engaging in conversational chats

---

LLMs are useful because they act as powerful "thinking and cooperative partners" that can process, synthesize, guide and generate information at incredible speeds. It reads multiple pages, understands it and gives you a customized direct answer which is used:

- To Save Time on Everyday Chores
- To Accelerate Learning
- To Brainstorm, Edit and Refine the Data
- To Naviagate about Technology as Learning to Code

<br>

> [!CAUTION]
> LLMs works on the prediction concept that is it predicts the next move. Sometimes they are confidently state things that are completely false. <br>
> YOU SHOULD NEVER RELY ON THEM !!!

---

<h2><b>🤔 Why I named it LLM-Ops ?</b></h2>

LLMOps (Large Language Model Operations) is the application usage of DevOps principles to the entire lifecycle of a LLM.
It is the practice of deploying, monitoring and maintaining LLMs in your production environment.
